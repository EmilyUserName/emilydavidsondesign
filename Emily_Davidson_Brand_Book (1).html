04
Brand Personality

Voice & Tone

Who Emily is
She figures it out. No team of ten. No perfect setup. Just the problem, the tools, and enough stubbornness to finish.
She doesn't quit mid-project. Construction. Acupuncture. Teaching. Development. Marine mechanics. She sees things through.
Genuinely warm. Maine-direct and kitten-kind. She'll tell you the truth and mean well by it.
Making things is her first language. Fine art, carpentry, code, food — the medium changes. The instinct doesn't.
Practical, not precious. She ships. Real users. Real products. Not pretty prototypes left in a drawer.
How Emily writes
Short sentences. Get to it. Maine bluntness is a feature, not a bug.
Concrete details. Real paying users. Maine-made. 2,957 organizations. Specificity earns trust.
Self-aware humor. She's allowed to be funny about herself. The brother quote lives here.
No corporate fluff. Synergy. Leverage. Circle back. Deliverables. These words are banned.
Italic for feeling. DM Serif Display italic carries her warmth and personality in headlines.
✓ Say this
"I ship practical things, not just pretty prototypes."
"Real paying users. Built lean. Distributed via marina flyers."
"My neighbors call me when the answer is complicated. My clients do too."
"Maine-made. Full-stack. Self-directed."
"I don't have an elevator pitch. I have calluses."
✗ Not this
"Passionate full-stack developer with proven expertise."
"Leveraging cutting-edge technologies to deliver results."
"I'm a hardworking team player who thinks outside the box."
"Results-oriented developer delivering seamless user experiences."
"Looking to utilize my skills in a dynamic environment."
"

"She is salty as the Atlantic. She is as kind as a kitten asleep in a patch of afternoon sun. And she is, without question, one of a kind."

— Written by someone who has known me my whole life, and still likes me

The kitten quote is a brand asset

This quote is a gift. It's funny, it's real, it's warm, and it's entirely original. Use it on the website, in cover letters, in email footers. It's the most human thing in the whole brand. Don't throw it away for a stock headline.

Portrait
Candid, natural light. No studio backdrops.
✓ Do use
→ Natural, overcast, or golden-hour light. Harsh flash is banned.
→ Real textures — wood, rope, rock, sea, paint, keyboard.
→ Slightly desaturated edits. Warm but not Instagram-warm.
→ Maine as a setting whenever possible. It's a differentiator.
→ Candid portraits. You working, thinking, doing something.
✗ Avoid
→ Stock photos of people in offices pretending to work.
→ Neon or overly saturated color grading.
→ Generic tech imagery — glowing keyboards, server rooms.
→ AI-generated illustrations unless clearly intentional.
→ Formal headshot style. You're not a consultant in a suit.
